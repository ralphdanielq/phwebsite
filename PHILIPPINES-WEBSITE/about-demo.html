Geography, Natural Resources, Seasons and Climate


The Philippines' yearly population growth rate from 2015 to 2020 was 1.63%. The country's population as of the 2020 census was 109,035,343[6]. The country's first census, which tallied 667,612 people, was conducted in the Philippines in 1591.


Tagalog and Cebuano are the indigenous languages most widely spoken, with 23.8 million (45 million as Filipino speakers) and 16 million speakers, respectively. Ilokano, Hiligaynon, Waray, different Bikol dialects, Kapampangan, Pangasinan, Maranao, Maguindanao, Kinaray-a, Zamboangueo, and Tausug are the other 11 indigenous languages with at least one million native speakers. More than 93% of people speak one or more of them as their mother tongue. Although there are between 120 and 170 unique indigenous languages spoken in the Philippines, Filipino and English are the official languages.
