PHILIPPINE CULTURE



Culture


East and West collide in the culture of the Philippines. The Filipinos have a distinctly Asian heritage and a significant Western tradition. Chinese traders, Spanish conquistadors, and American rulers all had an impact on how the contemporary Filipino culture evolved. Particularly toward Western guests, Filipinos have a reputation for being highly welcoming. Filipinos are emotive and enthusiastic about life in a way that looks more Latin than Asian because of their close links to Spanish culture. Contemporary artists' works, like Rody Herrera's painting Unahan Sa Duluhan, make this simple to perceive.



The core and most significant element of Filipino culture is the family. There is a ban on divorce, and annulments are uncommon. In tough economic times, the family serves as a safety net for people, especially elderly people. Frequently, children remain with their parents until they are adults and only leave when they are married. Family ties can have an impact on commercial and political relationships.


Pakikisama and Utang na Loob are two characteristics that several international tourists sometimes find challenging to comprehend. Pakikisama, which loosely translates as "getting along," calls on people to ignore minor wrongdoings or indiscretions in order to maintain harmony in their personal, professional, or familial relationships. Utang na Loob is the phrase used to describe the practice of returning favors. These characteristics highlight the value that Filipinos place on sustaining positive interpersonal connections and prioritizing the needs of the group over those of the individual.


Roman Catholics make up more than 80% of the population in the Philippines. Celebrations in honor of a town's patron saint are rather common. It is best to refrain from criticizing the Catholic church since it is not taken lightly.



People shake hands to welcome one another in the Philippines. "Hello" or "Yes" can be conveyed with a grin or arched eyebrows. A wave of the hand downwards might call someone.
