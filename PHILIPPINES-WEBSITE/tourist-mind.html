TOURIST ATTRACTIONS IN MINDANAO





Enchanted River





The famed Enchanted River, another mythical wonder tucked away in Hinatuan village in Surigao del Sur, is reputedly the bluest river in the nation. Although it is 290 meters long, many divers are still unsure about its depth.

>

Although diving excursions have been performed in the past, it is still unclear what is at the bottom of the mythical river. The Hinatuan Enchanted River opens out to an estuary where saline water combines with the river's spring water and flows into the Philippine Sea and the Pacific Ocean.


Only a little fraction of it is represented by the renowned blue river. At the deepest point of the river, divers even discovered the entrance to an underwater cave. Visitors are not permitted to swim in the main pool in order to protect its beauty. A dedicated swimming area is located about 10 meters from the main pool.



Samal Island





The largest resort city in the Philippines is the Island Garden City of Samal in Davao del Norte, often known as Samal Island. It is home to more than 30 resorts with more than 1,000 rooms. Samal Island, which has white sand beaches including Kaputian Beach, Vanishing Island, and Talikud Island, is home to colorful coral reefs and azure oceans.


You can cross the alleged Vanishing Island during low tide. You may go snorkeling or scuba diving at Talikud Island, or you can just laze on its expansive swath of fine white sand. Additionally, you may do mountain climbing on Mount Puting Bato and cave exploring at Baga, Magongawong, and Balete Caves.


Hagimit Falls offers swimming opportunities in its 3- to 7-foot deep cascades and natural pools. A large number of fruit-eating bats may then be seen by exploring Monfort Bat Cave.





Philippine Eagle Centre (Davao)





The Philippine Eagle Centre, an 8-hectare wildlife breeding facility in Davao City, is home to the critically endangered bird species known as the Philippine Eagle. It also houses the only captive eagles in the nation. The world-class park has enormous aviaries as well as smaller cages for various native species, as well as towering aviary duplexes mimicking those in Singapore Zoo.


This wildlife facility not only houses eagles but also macaques and crocodiles. The Philippine Eagle Centre has been informing visitors about the value of protecting the haribon and its environment for more than 20 years. There is a free guided tour of the wildlife center that includes eagle presentations.
