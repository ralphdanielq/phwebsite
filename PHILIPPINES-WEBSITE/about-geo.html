THE GEOGRAPHY & ENVIRONMENT



Geography, Natural Resources, Seasons and Climate


One of the world's biggest archipelagos is the Philippines. Its shoreline is more erratic and lengthier than the US coastline. This particular physical feature had a significant impact on both subsequent communities in the Philippines as well as earlier settlements' socioeconomic conditions. Along the coastline, trading stations were built. Some of these trading sites, such as Manila and Cebu, are now significant metropolitan centers in the Philippines. Fish can be found in abundance in freshwater lakes, rivers, and coastal locations.


There are around 2,000 different kinds of fish, and some of them are fished for profit. Banak (mullet), dalag (mudfish), bangus (milkfish), barile (tuna), banak (tuna), and dilis are some of these marine goods (anchovy). Other marine goods include edible seaweed, shells, corals, and pearls as well as suso (snails), alimango (crabs), hipon (shrimps), lobsters, and clams.




Mountains and volcanoes in the Philippines have shaped the country's topography. There are five significant mountain ranges on the island of Luzon: the Caraballo, Cordillera, Sierra Madre, Zambales, and Tagaytay. The Sierra Madre mountain range is found in the west-northwest corner of Luzon, while the Caraballo and Cordillera mountain ranges are found in the northern part of the island (Quezon, Aurora, Nueva Vizcaya, Cagayan provinces). The Tagaytay range is located in the southern Tagalog provinces of Cavite and Batangas, while the Zambales range is located in the provinces of Zambales and Bataan. There are four main mountain ranges on the island of Mindanao: Surigao, Butuan, Central-Western, and Western. The central-western range contains the tallest mountain, Mt. Apo (9,692 feet), which is situated there.




There are active volcanoes in all of the country's regions. The three most damaging eruptions from these volcanoes were caused by Mt. Pinatubo, Taal volcano, and Mt. Mayon. In 1990, Mt. Pinatubo's eruption buried villages, claimed the lives of hundreds of individuals, and left tens of thousands of people homeless. More than 1,300 people were murdered by the Taal volcano's eruption in 1911, and it again buried six barrios and killed hundreds of others in 1966. On the other side, these volcanoes have made the country extremely fruitful, and the produce of the soil has nourished the Filipino people throughout history, despite being insufficient at times.




The natural resources of the Philippines are abundant. Numerous products, including coconut, copra, bananas, pineapples, sugar, gum, resins, and rubber, are produced on its lush soil. These agricultural crops include several that are grown for export. The Central Plains of Luzon are primarily responsible for producing rice, which is the main meal of the Filipino people. Ironically, the Philippines imports rice since output frequently falls short of demand. The Philippines is home to a wide variety of plants and animals, many of which are endemic, or found only in the Philippines.
